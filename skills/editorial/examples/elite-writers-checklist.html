A Checklist for Better Prose

The Elite
Writer’s Checklist.

Run through these forty-four questions after you write. Each one is a small invitation to make a sentence sharper, a thought clearer, a piece of writing more its own.

Forty-four checks · Five movements For your next draft

How to use it

  1. i.Finish a draft. Walk away long enough that the words stop sounding like yours.
  2. ii.Read through each question in order. Tick only when you can honestly answer well.
  3. iii.Treat an unticked box as a small invitation to revisit a sentence, not a verdict.

01

Intent & audience

Before language, settle the why and the who.

  • Have I avoided pretentiousness throughout the writing?
  • Have I been sensitive to my readers’ views, assumptions, and familiarity with the subject?
  • Have I written with full awareness of my primary intent?
  • Have I aspired to make this piece of writing beautiful?
02

Concision

Cut what doesn’t earn its place on the page.

  • Have I avoided pointlessly fancy, flowery, and formal language?
  • Do I avoid using redundant or implied words?
  • Have I used any needlessly long words?
  • Do I unnecessarily repeat any ideas or points?
  • Have I made any simple points sound needlessly complicated?
  • Have I gone into too much detail, or given too many examples or explanations?
  • Do I use any double negatives without good reason?
  • Have I described any entities in a longwinded way?
  • Do any sentences carry thoughts that are too long, or any weighed down with subclauses?
  • Have I avoided pointless ceremony — capitalising words or putting quote marks around them unnecessarily?
03

Clarity

Make every sentence unmistakable on first reading.

  • Are any points in the writing fuzzy?
  • Does my writing reflect clear, logical thinking from beginning to end?
  • Will a reader’s mind go blank anywhere, or might someone be forced to reread?
  • Are all my sentences tightly focused?
  • Are all modifiers (words that alter others) properly placed?
  • Is there any ambiguity, anywhere at all, in the writing?
  • Are all comparisons crisp, all contrasts sharp, and all distinctions between words meaningful?
  • Do I have any unnecessarily “curly” sentences in the writing?
  • Do I avoid soul-killing jargon or buzzwords?
  • Are my tenses consistent enough throughout?
  • Do I refer to entities as “it” and “they” in an appropriate and consistent way?
  • Have I been clear about what any references to “this,” “that,” “they,” and “those” relate to?
  • Have I used the word “that” too often anywhere?
  • Does clutter obscure any of the key ideas in a sentence?
  • Do I stick to my “house style” throughout?
04

Flow & structure

Build music into how the piece moves.

  • Do I have a well-defined and elegant narrative structure?
  • Are all the points tied neatly into their narrative sections?
  • Are all my paragraphs ordered elegantly?
  • Do any sentences still sound clunky?
  • Does the writing flow like music from start to finish?
  • Do all my sentences transition elegantly?
  • Do multiple ideas in a sentence sit together in a parallel way?
  • Are there any ugly word echoes in the copy?
  • Do my sentences, and words, sound too “samey” anywhere?
05

Voice & style

Make it sing in a way nobody else could.

  • Are there any cliches or stale expressions in my copy?
  • Do I use any weak, filler, or hedging words that don’t add anything?
  • Do my sentences create enough pictures, feelings, or emotions in the reader?
  • Do I reverse into sentences too often?
  • Do I stick mostly to the active voice?
  • Does the writing have enough charisma?
